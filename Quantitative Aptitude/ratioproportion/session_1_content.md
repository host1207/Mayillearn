# RATIO & PROPORTION – SESSION 1 NOTES

## RATIO – DEFINITION

Ratio is the comparison of two or more quantities of the same kind by division.
(The units must be the same to compare.)

**Example:**
A : B = 1 : 2
This means B is twice A.



## PROPORTION – DEFINITION

Proportion is the equality of two ratios.
(Tanglish) Proportion na rendu ratios equal-aa irukkardhu.

**Standard form:**
a : b = c : d



## TYPES OF RATIOS

### 1) DUPLICATE RATIO

Duplicate ratio is obtained by squaring each term of the ratio.

**General form:**
a : b → a² : b²

**Example:**
3 : 7 has duplicate ratio of 9 : 49



### 2) TRIPLICATE RATIO

Triplicate ratio is obtained by cubing each term of the ratio.

**General form:**
a : b → a³ : b³

**Example:**
1 : 2 has triplicate ratio of 1 : 8



### 3) SUB-DUPLICATE RATIO

Sub-duplicate ratio is obtained by taking the square root of each term of the ratio.

**General form:**
a : b → √a : √b

**Example:**
1 : 4 has sub-duplicate ratio of 1 : 2



### 4) SUB-TRIPLICATE RATIO

Sub-triplicate ratio is obtained by taking the cube root of each term of the ratio.

**General form:**
a : b → ∛a : ∛b

**Example:**
1 : 8 has sub-triplicate ratio of 1 : 2



### 5) INVERSE RATIO

Inverse ratio is obtained by interchanging the terms of the ratio.

**General form:**
a : b → b : a

**Example:**
1 : 3 has inverse ratio of 3 : 1



## RATIO IN FRACTION FORM

A ratio can be written in fractional form, but it is usually expressed in the form a : b.
(Tanglish) Ratio fraction-la ezhudhalaam. Aanaa normally a : b form-la dhaan write pannuvom.



## CONVERTING A FRACTIONAL RATIO INTO A SIMPLE RATIO

**Example:**
a : b = 2/9 : 1/3

**Step 1:**
Find the LCM of the denominators.
LCM of 9 and 3 = 9

**Step 2:**
Multiply each term by the LCM.
2/9 × 9 = 2
1/3 × 9 = 3

**Step 3:**
Take only the numerators.

**Final ratio: 2 : 3**

(Tanglish) Denominator-oda LCM eduthu, rendu fraction-um multiply pannitu, numerators mattum eduthaa simple ratio varum.
