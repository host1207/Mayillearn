## Sunflower

Sunflowers are one of nature's most beautiful flowers. They are known for their large, bright blooms and their ability to track the sun across the sky.

### Key Facts about Sunflowers

| Feature | Detail |
|---|---|
| Scientific Name | Helianthus annuus |
| Native to | North America |
| Average Height | 1.5 to 3.5 metres |
| Lifespan | Annual |
| Uses | Oil, seeds, decoration |

Sunflowers are not just beautiful - they are also very **useful**. Sunflower seeds are eaten as snacks, and sunflower oil is one of the most widely used cooking oils in the world.

> Fun Fact: A single sunflower head is actually made up of up to 2,000 tiny flowers called florets!

```quiz
question: What is the colour of a sunflower?
A: Red
B: Blue
C: Green
D: Yellow
answer: D
explanation: Sunflowers are bright Yellow! Their large petals are yellow and their centre is dark brown.
```

### More about Sunflowers

The scientific name **Helianthus** comes from the Greek words *helios* (sun) and *anthos* (flower). Young sunflowers exhibit a behaviour called **heliotropism** - they literally follow the sun across the sky from east to west!
